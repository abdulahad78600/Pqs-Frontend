Regulatory Tailwinds

Aligned With the Future
of Cannabis Regulation

The Continuing Appropriations and Extensions Act of 2026 bans synthetic hemp-derived cannabinoids effective November 12, 2026 — accelerating our pathway to profitability and creating unprecedented market opportunity.

Federal Conversion Ban

The Continuing Appropriations and Extensions Act of 2026 excludes from "legal hemp" any cannabinoids that are "synthesised or manufactured outside the cannabis plant" — closing the loophole that allowed CBD to be converted into delta-8, delta-10, and other synthetic cannabinoids.

November 12, 2026 Deadline

A federal ban on intoxicating, hemp-derived products takes effect, restricting hemp products to no more than 0.4mg of total THC per container. Any product exceeding this limit becomes a Schedule I controlled substance.

~95% of Hemp Products Eliminated

This reclassification is expected to make roughly 95% of current hemp products illegal — dismantling the "gas station weed" market and forcing consumer demand back into fully licensed cannabis frameworks.
